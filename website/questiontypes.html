Question Types

P

Picture Multiple Choice

This question will pick a photo from your tree and ask you to choose who is shown in the picture.

You will receive a penalty for answering this question incorrectly.

R

Relationship Multiple Choice

This question calculates a relationship path between you and another person in the tree. You are then asked to select the correct person represented by this path. This question increases in difficulty by generating a longer relationship path.

You will receive a penalty for answering this question incorrectly.

T

Complete the Tree

Ancestor badges are shown in the large sign for 3 generations from your family tree. The smaller signs below represent a small family tree: the main person or child of the family belongs in the bottom box, the parents belong in the middle row of boxes, the grandparents fit in the top row of small boxes. For example, the smaller sign at the top left is for the main person's father's father. The top right sign is for the mother's mother. To complete this question drag the ancestor badges from the top sign to their correct place in the family tree. If a person is in the correct place, their sign will be outlined in yellow. If they are in the incorrect place it will be outlined in red.

Depending on the difficulty level of this question, some of the people may already be in their correct place when the question begins. There is no penalty for placing someone in the incorrect place except for the time it takes to remove them and place them somewhere else.

F

Fact Multiple Choice

This question chooses a event from a person in your tree. You must then select the person who experienced that event at the described place and time. There is guaranteed to only be one correct answer.

You will receive a penalty for answering this question incorrectly.

T

Timeline Sort

This question chooses a person from your tree and randomly sorts all of the facts for that person. To correctly complete the question, you must put the facts back into their correct order. You may reorder the facts by clicking and dragging them to a new spot.

When a fact is in its correct place, the date of the event and the age of the person at the time of the event will appear. This question gets more difficult by choosing a person with more facts.

M

Map the Facts

This question randomly chooses a person from your tree and asks you to place all of their facts into their correct locations on a map. To place a fact on the map, drag it from the top into the correct place on the map. If you try to place a fact in the incorrect place, it will move back to the top. Use the standard Google map controls to pan around and zoom in and out on the map. Each location is guaranteed to be unique and there will only be one fact per place.

Some places may be too close to distinquish at the current zoom level and will be shown as a cluster with a number. Zoom in to that location until the unique places are visible in order to drag facts to locations that were in that cluster. As you zoom out on the map, locations will merge into a cluster again.

This question gets more difficult by choosing a person with more facts.

B

Map People by Birth Place

In this question, you will place people on a map by their correct birth place. To do this, simply drag them from the top area down into their correct place on the map. Each place is guaranteed to be unique, and there will only be one person born in a certain place.

Some places may be too close to distinquish at the current zoom level and will be shown as a cluster with a number. Zoom into that location until the unique places are visible in order to drag people to the locations that were in that cluster. As you zoom out on the map, locations will merge into a cluster again.

This question gets more difficult by adding more people to place.
